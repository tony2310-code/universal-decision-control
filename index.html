Universal Decision Control System

Local-first. Stored on this device only. Export weekly. Storage: local Guide

How to use (fast, but not confusing)

This is a 1 to 7 checklist. Start at step 1. End at step 7. Then hit Save.

After every Save, the app prints a Decision Breakdown at the top. That is your simple explanation of what just happened.

If your intensity is 7 or higher, you must enter an Action and a Deadline. That is the “no escape hatch” rule.

One full example (copy this if you want)
1 Pattern: Avoidance Loop
2 Trigger: I opened my outreach list at 10:00. I scrolled for 6 minutes. I did not message anyone.
3 Thought: If I get ignored, it proves this is not working.
4 Emotion: Anxiety. Intensity 8 before. 5 after.
5 Reframe: Avoiding makes the fear bigger. One message shrinks the fear.
6 Action: Send 1 message now. Stop after 1.
Deadline: Today at 12:30.
7 Result: I sent it. Nothing exploded. I felt calmer.
Evidence: Screenshot of sent message.
Scoreboard: Conversations = 1.
Identity: I do one uncomfortable rep even when anxious.
Default is now. You can change it.
1
Pattern
Help and example
Pick the pattern that matches what you actually did. Not what you wish you did.
If you pick a pattern, the app will prefill a suggested reframe and action. You can edit them.
Example: You delayed a hard task by scrolling. That is usually Avoidance Loop.
2
Trigger
Help and example
Write what a camera would record. No meaning, no conclusions.
Example: I sent a message at 9:02. No reply by 11:00.
3
Automatic thought
Help and example
Write the first thought that hit you. Keep it raw. Do not fix it yet.
Example: This is pointless. I will fail anyway.
4
Emotion and intensity
Help and example
Intensity is how strong the feeling is right now. 0 is none. 10 is peak.
Belief is how much you believe the thought. 0 means you do not buy it. 100 means you fully buy it.
Example: Thought is “I will fail.” Belief before 80. After reframing, belief after 45.
Rule: if intensity before is 7 or higher, Action and Deadline are required.
5
Reframe
Help and example
One calm sentence grounded in facts that points toward your next move. Not a pep talk.
If you cannot write a reframe, write a rule instead.
Example reframe: The emotion is real, but it does not predict the outcome.
Example rule: One rep beats zero reps.
6
Action and deadline
Help and example
Action must be small enough that you can do it even if you still feel bad.
Deadline must be specific. Not “today.” Use an actual time.
Bad: “Work on it later.”
Good: “Send one message by 2:30 PM.”
7
Result and evidence
Help and example
Result is what happened. Evidence is the proof object you could show someone.
Scoreboard is the number that matters. Identity is the sentence that makes it stick.
Result: I published the post.
Evidence: Screenshot of the post with time.
Scoreboard: Conversations = 1.
Identity: I act even when uncertain.

Quick prompts
Use one prompt, then take one small action.
  • What is the fact, and what story am I adding?
  • What would I tell a friend who said this thought?
  • What is one small proof I can create in the next 2 hours?
  • What is the smallest next step that still counts?
  • What rule would I follow if I wanted less regret later?
